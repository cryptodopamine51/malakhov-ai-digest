## Repeated Blocks in SERP

- Generic definitions of artificial intelligence in industry.
- "Top use cases" lists: quality control, predictive maintenance, planning, energy optimization, safety.
- Vendor promises around Industry 4.0 and digital factories.
- Case lists without baseline metrics.
- Broad "AI transformation" language that does not help choose a first pilot.

## Missing Blocks for a Business Reader

- Diagnostic for selecting the first production process.
- What data is enough for a pilot and what is not enough.
- How to calculate baseline and payback before building a model.
- False-positive / false-negative cost for quality control.
- Human review and change management for inspectors, operators and maintenance staff.
- Russian constraints: personal data in video, data storage, integration with 1С/MES/ERP, limited internal AI specialists.

## Malakhov AI Differentiation

- More practical: choose a pilot by loss type, not by AI technology name.
- More local: mention 152-ФЗ, Russian operational systems and the fact that many companies will not have a dedicated AI team.
- More honest about limits: AI is not the first step if there is no baseline, stable process, camera/sensor/log data or accountable process owner.
- Better tables/checklists: scenario matrix, data readiness checklist, ROI calculation, "when not to start" block.

## Anti-Copying Notes

- Do not reuse competitor phrasing.
- Use references only to understand intent, depth, and questions.
- Facts and numbers must go into `03-source-notes.md` with source/date/risk.
