## Reference Patterns

| Source type | Repeated blocks | Weak spots | Use for our article |
|---|---|---|---|
| Integrator/vendor posts | broad ranges, chatbot pricing, CRM integrations | often sales-oriented; hides support and data work | identify common price anchors |
| Cloud/model pricing pages | token prices, packages, limits, billing units | does not explain implementation TCO | cite only concrete tariffs |
| Research/media | adoption, failed POCs, market spend | not operational enough for SMB budgeting | explain why budget discipline matters |
| Automation platforms | monthly plans and execution/credit limits | not enough for total implementation cost | show that tool subscription is one line item |

## What Competitors Usually Cover

- Price of chatbot development.
- Price of API usage.
- Subscription costs.
- Rough implementation packages.
- Generic ROI language.

## What Is Usually Missing

- Difference between pilot and production.
- Cost of data preparation.
- Cost of human review and quality control.
- Cost of logs, rights, fallback and support.
- Stop/go criteria for pilot.
- Russia/CIS context and local provider tariffs.

## Malakhov AI Differentiation

- Give budget ranges as planning bands, not as a fake universal price.
- Show TCO and monthly operating cost.
- Include hidden expenses and cost-control mechanics.
- Link to existing implementation guide to avoid duplicating the full AI adoption plan.
